Ingredients


* 3 cups ripe mango

* 2 cups of sugar

* Water

* Ascorbic acido

preparation

1. Cut the handle in thirds. Remove the pulp with a fork or peel it beforehand.

2. Cook the mango with the sugar in a saucepan for 25 to 40 minutes or until it thickens. The color should be darker than the original yellow, but not darker than a medium orange.

3. Crush the handle. With a fork or large spoon, mash it until it acquires jam consistency.

4. Dissolve the ascorbic acid in hot water, and add it to the mango and sugar mixture.

5. Procuse it and put it in canned food according to the US Department of Agriculture's conservation guidelines. (USDA).

6. For every 230 to 290 g or 1 small jar of mango jam, you will have to add about 4 to 6 times more than 500 mg of ascorbic acid, since you will not be using any artificial preservative. If the average sugar is 1: 1 (1 cup of mango for 1 cup of sugar), then you can keep it for 5 to 6 months without refrigeration. Those who are more careful with health may prefer to use a smaller proportion, but their life will be shorter too.

7. If you keep it in a freezer, it will stay in good condition for 2 years (unopened); if you open it, 1 year.
